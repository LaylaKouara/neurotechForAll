Making Neurotechnology Fairer and More Inclusive

Exploring and addressing the barriers that prevent marginalised communities from fully benefiting from brain health technologies.

About

This project explores how neurotechnologies can be developed and applied more equitably. It focuses on ensuring these technologies are inclusive, accessible, and responsive to the needs of diverse populations.

We aim to identify structural and technical barriers that may limit fair participation and access, and to support the design of approaches that address these limitations from the outset. Our work brings together different forms of evidence and perspectives to inform best practices across research, development, and implementation.

Focus

We are committed to understanding the broader social, cultural, and technical factors that influence the use and impact of neurotechnologies. Our research considers how to support more inclusive development processes and outcomes across the field.

By promoting collaboration between researchers, communities, and practitioners, we aim to contribute to a more reflective and responsible approach to innovation in brain-related technologies.
